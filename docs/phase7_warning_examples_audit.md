# Phase 7 Warning Examples Audit

## Purpose

This follow-up audits representative examples behind the 8,206 Phase 7
warnings and checks whether the policy in
`docs/phase7_warning_resolution_policy.md` is supported by concrete chunks.
It does not resolve warnings or authorize data, validator, cleaner, or chunker
changes.

## Inputs

- `/tmp/processed_jsonl_validation_report.json`, generated by the official
  Phase 7 CLI on June 10, 2026.
- `data/processed/legal_chunks.jsonl`, scanned read-only.
- `docs/phase7_warning_resolution_policy.md`.
- `docs/phase7_processed_chunk_validation_plan.md`.

The CLI report returned exit code 0 and retained the expected
`pass_with_warnings` status.

## Sampling Method

The audit combined the complete `warning_distribution_summary` with a
read-only scan of all 40,389 JSONL rows. The scan:

- grouped text shorter than 50 and 20 characters by chunk kind and repeal
  metadata;
- selected Point, Clause, Article, `BLHS_VBHN`, repealed, and non-repealed
  examples;
- located each configured authority marker in `text` and `parent_text`;
- inspected short direct-marker chunks for signature/footer-like content;
- kept previews short and did not write analysis output into the repository.

The validator uses raw character length and exempts metadata-marked
empty/repealed chunks from short-text warnings. The direct scan found 4,821
raw texts below 50 characters: 4,645 non-repealed warning chunks and 176
metadata-marked repealed chunks. A whitespace-trimmed exploratory count is
slightly higher and is not used as the official warning total.

## Short-Text Warning Examples

### Observations

- Official short-text warnings comprise 3,605 Points, 1,039 Clauses, and one
  Article.
- The highest-volume law, `BLHS_VBHN`, is dominated by concise penalty,
  condition, and enumeration Points rather than malformed chunks.
- The sampled short units retain precise citation and hierarchy anchors, while
  `parent_text` supplies the Article context needed for interpretation.
- The sole short Article sample is a complete constitutional proposition, not
  truncated text.

## Authority-Marker Warning Examples

### Observations

- All 3,561 authority-marker warning chunks contain a marker in
  `parent_text`.
- 3,019 are parent-only; 542 also contain the marker in direct `text`.
  No warning chunk was text-only.
- `BỘ TRƯỞNG` accounts for 3,935 field incidences: 502 in `text` and 3,433
  in `parent_text`.
- `CHỦ NHIỆM` has 178 incidences and `CHỦ TỊCH QUỐC HỘI` has 123.
- No exact `TM. QUỐC HỘI` or `KT. BỘ TRƯỞNG` occurrence was found in the
  current corpus scan.
- The shortest sampled direct `BỘ TRƯỞNG` chunks are operative delegations
  such as “Bộ trưởng ... quy định chi tiết Điều này,” not signature lines.

### Representative Examples

| Marker / field | Citation | Preview | Judgment |

### Interpretation

The sampled markers are mainly legal authority references or repeated parent
Article context. A lexical marker cannot distinguish these from a signature
block by itself. Current warning-only severity is appropriate. Phase 8 context
selection may avoid returning unrelated parent Clauses, but it must not remove
operative delegations from direct `text`.

## Suspected Real Issues

No strong cleaner/chunker defect was found in the sampled examples:

- no direct chunk consisted only of a bare authority title;
- the shortest direct-marker chunks expressed operative rulemaking authority;
- parent-only markers came from valid sibling provisions in the same Article;
- no sampled preview showed repeated administrative footer text or a signature
  block dominating the embedding unit;
- Phase 7 still reports zero hard contamination failures.

This does not prove that every warning is harmless. A future follow-up should
target direct-marker outliers using ratios such as marker position, signature
vocabulary, and proportion of non-propositional text. Cleaner/chunker changes
require that stronger direct-text evidence.

## Policy Check Against W1

The examples support the W1 policy.

- Short Point and Clause chunks should remain warning-only and retain their
  hierarchy/citation anchors.
- Metadata-marked repealed units are expected traceability records.
- Generic authority markers should remain warning-only because sampled direct
  occurrences are substantive legal provisions.
- Parent-only marker warnings are better handled by later context selection or
  compression than by corpus mutation.
- No automatic deletion, merging, threshold weakening, or warning suppression
  is justified by this audit.

## Recommended Next Actions

1. Accept the W1 warning policy without changing Phase 7 rules.
2. Preserve all 8,206 warnings and their distribution in the audit trail.
3. Before production indexing, manually review a stratified set of direct-text
   marker outliers, especially unusually short or signature-like units.
4. In later approved Phase 8 work, evaluate child-only embedding with cited
   parent-Article expansion and selective context compression.
5. Add retrieval evaluation cases for concise but decisive Points and Clauses.
6. Require concrete direct-text examples and regression tests before any
   cleaner, chunker, threshold, or severity change.

## Non-Goals

This audit does not:

- modify processed chunks, hierarchy files, or generated reports;
- resolve, suppress, reclassify, or recount warnings;
- change thresholds, marker lists, or validator behavior;
- authorize cleaner/chunker changes;
- implement embedding, indexing, retrieval, context compression, or RAG;
- assess substantive legal validity or provide legal advice.
